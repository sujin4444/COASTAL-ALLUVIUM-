COASTAL ALLUVIUM SOIL – TAMIL NADU

Districts:

Tenkasi, Tirunelveli, Thoothukudi, Virudhunagar, Madurai, Sivagangai, Ramanathapuram

(Ref: TNAU Agritech Portal)

Description

Coastal alluvium soil is a sandy to sandy-loam soil formed by deposition of river and marine sediments along coastal plains and estuaries. It generally has low water-holding capacity, shallow to moderate depth, and may exhibit salinity in areas close to the seashore.

Soil Properties of Coastal Alluvium Soil

  1. Texture: Sandy to sandy-loam
  2. Drainage: Well to excessively drained
  3. Water-holding capacity: Low to moderate
  4. Soil reaction (pH): Neutral to slightly alkaline (≈7.0–8.5)
  5. Salinity: Moderate to high near coastal belts
  6. Organic matter: Low content
  7. Fertility status: Moderate; deficient in nitrogen
  8. Groundwater table: Generally shallow
  9. Soil colour: Light brown to greyish

Crops Cultivated

  • Tenkasi: Paddy
  • Tirunelveli: Paddy, vegetables, casuarina
  • Thoothukudi: Paddy, coconut
  • Virudhunagar: Paddy
  • Madurai: Paddy, vegetables
  • Sivagangai: Paddy
  • Ramanathapuram: Paddy, pulses, salt-tolerant crops
